// WHAT THIS IS BASED ON
01 // THE CHC FRAMEWORK In psychometrics - the scientific study of cognitive ability - there is one framework that everything else is built on. The Cattell-Horn-Carroll model. It is what modern IQ tests use. It is what educational psychologists use when they assess children. It breaks human cognitive ability into measurable strata: fluid intelligence, crystallised intelligence, working memory capacity, retrieval fluency.

Your six scores map directly onto those strata. When Dr. Daley asks you a question, she is not winging it.
02 // THE RESEARCH There is a growing body of peer-reviewed evidence that habitual AI use is changing how people think. Not in a vague, op-ed, "what does this mean for society" way. In a measurable, specific, this-is-happening-in-your-brain way.

Kosmyna et al. (2025): cognitive engagement 55% lower during AI-assisted tasks. Fan et al. (2025): metacognitive laziness - the gradual erosion of the impulse to notice whether you are actually thinking. Doshi and Hauser (2024): significant reduction in creative novelty in AI-assisted groups. Gloria Mark: average sustained focus now 47 seconds. Sparrow et al.: the Google Effect - the documented tendency to stop retaining information you know you can retrieve later.
03 // CONVERSATIONAL ASSESSMENT The reason this is a phone call and not a form is that self-report is useless. If you ask someone whether they have a good memory, they will tell you what they believe, which is not the same as what is true.

Cognitive competencies reveal themselves in how people speak under light pressure - in real time, unrehearsed, with nowhere to hide. You cannot perform your way through a working memory task. You cannot fake fluid reasoning when someone is waiting for your answer.
